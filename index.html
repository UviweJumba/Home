I.Exordium

In the beginning was the proof, and the proof was with logic, and the proof was sound. Without it nothing that compiles was made.

Theo Ashford is a mathematician and computer scientist working where graph theory, computational geometry, and machine learning theory meet — the territory where structure has to survive translation: from proof to program, from continuous to discrete, from geometry to code.

The work here follows one long-standing question: which properties of a system are the ones that persist no matter how you choose to represent it? Recent efforts have looked for answers in spectral graph methods, in discrete curvature, and in what neural networks keep and discard when they compress the world into weights.

Before any of that, there was a proof set badly in a first-year notebook, and a professor who circled it in red — not because it was wrong, but because it could be shorter. That instinct, for the shortest true path between assumption and conclusion, has not gone away since.

arXiv · 2026

We establish tight concentration bounds for the spectral gap of sparse random hypergraphs under a generalized configuration model, extending prior results from graphs to higher-order structures and giving a cleaner route to expansion guarantees used elsewhere in this body of work.
